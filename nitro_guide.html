Alesis Nitro Drum Module

Quick Practice Guide

1. Basics

KITCycles through the module's built-in drum kit sounds. Turn/press to browse, use PAGE/SELECT or the dial to confirm.
VOLUMESets the overall output level of the module (pads, click, and songs together). Turn to match headphone or amp level.
DRUM OFFMutes the drum pads while keeping the click and/or song audio playing — useful for listening back without your playing covering the track.

2. Metronome Practice

CLICKToggles the built-in metronome on/off. A steady click keeps your internal tempo honest.
TEMPOHold and turn the dial, or use the ▲▼ arrows, to set the click speed in BPM (beats per minute).

Tip: Start slow with a click on every beat, then gradually increase the tempo a few BPM at a time as the pattern feels solid.

3. Play-Along with Built-in Songs

SONGLoads the module's built-in play-along tracks for practicing to real songs.
◀ ▶ / center ▮Navigate between songs and play/pause the currently loaded track.
PLAY/PRACTICEMutes an individual drum part (e.g., snare or hi-hat) from the song so you play that part live while the rest of the track continues.

4. Rhythm/Pattern Practice (Learning Mode)

PATTERN / RHYTHM / BEATLoads guided rhythm exercises that walk through beats and patterns at increasing difficulty.
PAGE/SELECTScrolls through the list of available exercises within Learning Mode.

5. Pad Layout Reference

Use this map to find which pad triggers which sound when editing kits or following pattern exercises.

CRASH 1 / CRASH 2Crash cymbal pads, outer edges of the kit.
RIDERide cymbal pad, upper right.
TOM 1–4Rack and floor tom pads, arced across the middle.
HI-HAT / HI-HAT CONTROLHi-hat pad and its open/closed pedal control, lower left.
SNARE / RIMSnare pad and rim-click zone, center.
KICKKick drum trigger, large and centered at the base of the kit.

6. Recording Yourself

RECORDArms and captures your playing (with click, song, or pattern if active) for later playback.
SAVEStores the recording so it isn't lost when the module powers off or a new kit is loaded.
START/STOPBegins and ends both recording and playback.

Tip: Play back every recording and check it against the click — this is the fastest way to spot rushing, dragging, or uneven fills.

7. Suggested Practice Routine

  1. Pick a kit — cycle KIT to a sound you like.
  2. Set the click — turn on CLICK and set a comfortable TEMPO.
  3. Warm up — run through basic rudiments on the pads with the click.
  4. Play along — switch to SONG mode and use PLAY/PRACTICE to mute one part you want to isolate.
  5. Record and review — hit RECORD, play, then SAVE and listen back to check your timing.
